About Me

I am a former aspiring movie-maker who has enjoyed working various low-level jobs in the film industry, primarily as a "background actor"(extra) and occasional production assisstant, during six long, strange years of rejection from film schools.

In 2014, I acquired steady, long-term employment for BookMasters Ltd, a Toronto-based independent bookseller which owns the Book City chain of retailers. This new stability provided me a foundation to go back to school - adult high school - at age 25, as I was now interested in pursuing a cellular biology degree and required many science and math prequisites I'd originally avoided as an aspiring arts student.

During a compressed two-year process beginning with relearning 9th-grade fractions and the periodic table and finishing with credits in 12th-Grade calculus, advanced functions, biology and chemistry, I rediscovered my childhood love of science and non-subjective, non-arts-centric student life. In the process, I developed a interest in coding through online courses on Codeacademy and Udemy, as well as an interest in the programmer lifestyle as I watched several close friends leave adult careers in varying fields to attend web development bootcamps and begin newer, happier careers and work-life balances in the world of programming. Thus, I am now here at age 29 with the hopes of having my cake and eating it too - finally getting to experience life as a university student at my dream-campus of U of T St. George, but "graduating" in time to have a career-ready skillset, and maybe even a career(!), in less time than it'll take to hit 30.
